Why liposomal iron is better than regular iron

Regular iron salts often irritate the gastrointestinal tract and can cause nausea, heaviness, or constipation—especially in women with sensitive digestion or when iron is taken on an empty stomach. Liposomal iron is designed differently: the active ingredient is placed inside a lipid “capsule” (liposome). This helps iron travel more gently through the digestive environment and may improve tolerability.

In practice, the goal is simple: raise hemoglobin and ferritin while keeping daily intake comfortable. Consistency matters more than aggressive dosing. A stable routine (for example, 1 capsule/day) can be easier to maintain and often leads to better real-life outcomes than irregular high-dose attempts.

Myths vs facts: “Iron always causes constipation” is a myth. Some forms and doses do, but it’s not inevitable. Another myth is that “more is faster” — excess iron may cause side effects and reduce adherence. A pharmaceutical approach means choosing a form that matches the patient’s lifestyle, risk factors, and comfort.

Hand Milk: pharmaceutical approach to care

Dry, cracked hands are not “just cosmetic”—they’re a barrier problem. Cold air, detergents, frequent handwashing, and low humidity disrupt the skin’s lipid layer. A pharmaceutical mindset focuses on restoring that barrier: hydration + lipids + comfort, without an unpleasant sticky feel that makes people stop using the product.

Hand Milk with Laminaria is positioned as a daily protective ritual. The idea is a soft texture that absorbs quickly yet stays present. If a product feels elegant, people use it more regularly—which is exactly how you get visible results. In winter, consistent application after washing and before going outside is especially effective.

Myths vs facts: “Only thick creams work” is a myth. Texture isn’t the only signal of efficacy. A balanced formula can be lightweight and still supportive, as long as it delivers barrier-friendly components and fits the user’s routine.
